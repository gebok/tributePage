Utula Utuoc Samana

A Papua New Guinea government official and Morobe's kwila-post leader

Under Premier Utula Samana, Morobe invested extensively in connecting rural areas and making access to government goods and services easier. His good work was, however, short lived.

Background

Samana, Utula Utuoc was born in 1948 in Lae, Morobe Province, Papua New Guinea

Education

He graduated with Bachelor of Arts from the University of Papua New Guinea in 1974, majoring in politics and public administration.

Career

Project officer social planning division national planning office, Department Financial and Planning, 1974-1975; tutor, U. Papua New Guinea, 1976; provincial planner, Morobe Provincial Government, 1978-1979; premier, Morobe Provincial Government, 1980-1987; founder, leader, Morobe Indiana Group leader, Melanesian United Front member, Fourth National Parliament for Morobe Province, 1987; minister for public service, minister for agriculture, Government Papua New Guinea, 1987-1988; minister education, Government Papua New Guinea, 1990-1994; permanent representative Papua New Guinea, United Nations, New York City, 1994-1998; head agriculture and livestock, Government of Papua New Guine, since 1998.
