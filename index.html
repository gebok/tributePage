Utula Utuoc Samana

A Papua New Guinea government official and Morobe's kwila-post leader

Under Premier Utula Samana, Morobe invested extensively in connecting rural areas and making access to government goods and services easier. His good work was, however, short-lived.

Background

Samana, Utula Utuoc was born in 1948 in Lae, Morobe Province, Papua New Guinea

Education

He graduated with a Bachelor of Arts from the University of Papua New Guinea in 1974, majoring in politics and public administration.

Career

1974-1975 -- Project Officer Social Planning Division, National Planning Office, Department Financial and Planning
1976 -- Tutor, University of Papua New Guinea
1978-1979 -- Provincial Planner, Morobe Provincial Government
1980-1987 -- Premier, Morobe Provincial Government
1987 -- Founder, leader, Morobe Indiana Group leader, Melanesian United Front member, Fourth National Parliament for Morobe Province
1987-1988 -- Minister for Public Service, Minister for Agriculture, Government Papua New Guinea
1990-1994 -- Minister for Education, Government Papua New Guinea
1994-1998 -- Permanent representative Papua New Guinea, United Nations, New York City
1998 -- Head Agriculture and Livestock, Government of Papua New Guinea

Utula Utuoc Samana has been listed as a noteworthy Papua New Guinea government official by Marquis Who's Who
